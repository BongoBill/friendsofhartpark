3 artists and one musical group to be in the Hart Hall during the Powwow.

 

(all artists' links below open up a new window)

 

Come enjoy the works of internationally know American Indian artists, Bernie Granados (painter/sculptor), Peggy Fontinont (photographer/bead artist), and Valena Dismukes (photographer/writer/found-objects art specialist).

  • Peggy will demonstrate beadwork and display her photos, with an emphasis on her non-profit group "The Living Wall" which honors all of the soldiers, highlighting American Indians, who fought in the Vietnam War.

  • Valena will have her book The Red-Black Connection on hand to sell and sign.

They will all be demonstrating their remarkable artwork during the powwow.

 

Also!

  • American Indian Recording artists, The Miracle Dolls will be performing on Saturday inside Hart Hall.

What?
It is with great pleasure that we invite you to experience a significant slice of Native American culture at the 17th Annual Hart of the West Powwow. We hope you will come and enjoy the dancers, drummers and the numerous displays of arts and crafts.

All Drums and Dancers Welcome!
Princess Contest!

The efforts of many have gone into making this a successful event. Among them, Native Americans who have traveled great distances to share part of their heritage with you, the Friends of Hart Park, the Department of Parks and Recreation, and all our sponsors. Come and enjoy Los Angeles County's largest Pow Wow.  Free admission!
