Chaplin Fest!
 

Hart-Chaplin trivia:  Mary Pickford, Douglas Fairbanks, and Chaplin formulated plans to open and manage their own distribution company, and in early 1919, they approached William S. Hart and asked him to partner with them. He did consider the venture — he even met with the group several times to discuss the new company’s plans — but his loyalty to Adolph Zukor’s studio won out, and he ultimately pulled out of the project. Even though Bill was not a founding member of what became United Artists, we loved celebrating him with this truly amazing, and historical event!

The Friends of Hart Park and Museum are people in your community who volunteer their time and talents to support the museum facilities.  For many years, funds raised by the Friends have been used to help maintain the mansion, help with the animals in the park, and clean/restore numerous art treasures.

This site is provided for your enjoyment, and all the proceeds from this site go to this ongoing support effort. Thank you for visiting us here!
