Hart-Chaplin trivia:  Mary Pickford, Douglas Fairbanks, and Chaplin formulated plans to open and manage their own distribution company, and in early 1919, they approached William S. Hart and asked him to partner with them. He did consider the venture — he even met with the group several times to discuss the new company’s plans — but his loyalty to Adolph Zukor’s studio won out, and he ultimately pulled out of the project. Even though Bill was not a founding member of what became United Artists, we look forward to celebrating him with this truly amazing, and historical event!

Hart Mansion Re-Opens

After successful renovation project, former

home of silent film star re-opens for tours just

in time for the holidays

After a much-needed renovation project, the Hart

Museum’s mansion has re-opened

Wednesday, December 22, 2010

 

Sporting a new roof and shutters, as well as

refurbished doors and window frames, the Hart

Museum will be open for normal hours of operation:

Wednesday—Friday Saturday and Sunday

10:00am—12:30pm 11:00am—3:30pm

Tours start every half an hour.

 
 

The Friends of Hart Park and Museum are people in your community who volunteer their time and talents to support the museum facilities.  For many years, funds raised by the Friends have been used to help maintain the mansion, help with the animals in the park, and clean/restore numerous art treasures.

This site is provided for your enjoyment, and all the proceeds from this site go to this ongoing support effort. Thank you for visiting us here!
