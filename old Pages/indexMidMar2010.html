Thrilling News!

The Post Office recently announced a new series of stamps honoring four western stars.  The stamps will feature our favorite cowboy, William S. Hart!

Click here for details on how William S. Hart park will celebrate on April 17th, and how you can join in the fun!

The stamps' images were created by illustrator Robert Rodriquez.  Mr. Rodriquez based his image of Hart on this classic movie poster from Bill Hart's last (and likely best) movie, Tumbleweeds.

For the announcement by the Pony Express, er, the US Post Office, click on the stamps at the top of the page, or here.

To learn more about the artist, click on his image below, or here.

The stamps will be available April 17th, so see you at Hart Park for the unveiling!

Jazz up your evening with harpist Lori Andrews who was described by Scott Yanow of “The All Music Guide to Jazz” as a musician who “shows that her delicate instrument can be used to produce swinging, soulful, funky and stomping music.”

Stamp Unveiling Ceremony

Saturday, April 17, 2010 at 10:00am 

Join us for the unveiling of the William S. Hart Stamp at the Hart Museum!  Hear about the Hart Stamp and Bill Hart’s legacy from dignitaries representing the Natural History Museum of Los Angeles County, Los Angeles County Department of Parks and Recreation, Friends of Hart Park, United States Postal Service and representatives from the Federal, State and City governments may also be present.  Light refreshments will be served courtesy of the William S. Hart Museum, a member of the Natural History Family of Museums. 

Directly following the ceremony, all four stamps within the Cowboys of the Silver Screen set will be available for purchase and cancellation through the United States Postage Service.  Check out the Friends of Hart Park’s one-of-a-kind envelopes designed specifically for the Hart Stamp Unveiling! 

Make an entire day’s visit at the Hart Park and Museum!  In honor of the Hart Stamp’s first day of issue, there will be plenty of music and good family fun.

For more information, click here or contact the Hart Museum at 661-254-4584.

The Friends of Hart Park and Museum are people in your community who volunteer their time and talents to support the museum facilities.  For many years, funds raised by the Friends have been used to help maintain the mansion, help with the animals in the park, and clean/restore numerous art treasures.

This site is provided for your enjoyment, and all the proceeds from this site go to this ongoing support effort. Thank you for visiting us here!
