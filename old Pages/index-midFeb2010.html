Thrilling News!

The Post Office recently announced a new series of stamps honoring four western stars.  The stamps will feature our favorite cowboy, William S. Hart!

The images were created by illustrator Robert Rodriquez.  Mr. Rodriquez based his image of Hart on this classic movie poster from Bill Hart's last (and likely best) movie, Tumbleweeds.

For the announcement by the Pony Express, uh, the US Post Office, click on the stamps at the top of the page, or here.

To learn more about the artist, click on his image below, or here.

The stamps will be available April 17th, so see you in line at the post office!

2nd Annual Black History Month Celebration

February 20, 11am-5pm and

February 21, 11am-4pm

Free to the public, in Hart Hall

Come enjoy the talents of Black and non-Black artists who created works dealing with African-American themes in history and culture!  This celebration features historical exhibits produced by Albert L. Ewing whose "Genesis Project I" is a photographic overview of the African-American experience.

The Friends of Hart Park and Museum are people in your community who volunteer their time and talents to support the museum facilities.  For many years, funds raised by the Friends have been used to help maintain the mansion, help with the animals in the park, and clean/restore numerous art treasures.

This site is provided for your enjoyment, and all the proceeds from this site go to this ongoing support effort. Thank you for visiting us here!
